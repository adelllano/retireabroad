COST OF LIVING

The cost of living and rent indices for each country uses New York City (NYC) as the base cost index. This means that New York City's index is set to 100, and the countries listed here are compared to NYC's cost of living and rent index. In this first chart, Malta shows a Cost Living Index of 60, the highest of the 12 countries listed. This indicates that on average, Malta's cost of living is 40% less that in NYC.

HEALTHCARE

To determine the healthcare index for each country, Numbeo.com visitors enter information based on a set of related questions. The questions pertain to the quality of healthcare, such as the skill/competency of medical staff, speed and accuracy in completing paperwork, availability of modern equipment, friendliness of staff, etc. The index is shown on a scale of 0 to 100, with 100 indicating a high level of satisfaction with the quality of healthcare.

CRIME

To determine the crime index for each country, Numbeo.com visitors enter information based on a set of related questions. The questions pertain to how the individual perceives the severity of the level of crime in their country. Visitors rate how they feel about the crime level over the past three years, feeling of safety while walking in the daytime vs the nightime, how much they worry about being robbed or having their car stolen, etc. The index is shown on a scale of 0 to 100, with 20-40 indicating low crime levels, 40-60 being moderate, and 60-80 as high .

LIFE EXPECTANCY

Life expectancy is typically determined by the average number of years a person is expected to live if mortality rates remain constant. The World Health Organization provides an average life expectancy at age 60 which is based on the mortality rate level of a country's population of 60 years of age. The United Stated has an average life expectancy of 84 at age 60. When comparing life expectancies between countries, keep in mind that less developed countries often don't have accurate reporting due to limited resources.
